How does good sleep affect our life?

Sleep helps us retain recently gained knowledge and developed skills.

Sleep helps us to pay attention, make decisions, and be creative.

Sleep helps maintain a healthy balance of the hormones that make you feel hungry (ghrelin) or full (leptin).

Sleep affects how our bodies reacts to insulin, which lowers the risk of getting diabetes.

Sleep supports healthy growth and development in children and teens, playing a role in puberty.

Sleep encourages a healthy immune system, vital for fighting off harmful substances.

Sleep helps to regulate our emotions and think clearer.
